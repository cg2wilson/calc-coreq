Example 3.2.7.
Video 4 
The solutions of \(2x^2-7x+5=0\) are the same thing as the zeros/x-intercepts of \(f(x)=2x^2-7x+5\text{.}\) We can find these solutions/zeros/intercepts graphically.
We can also find the solution to something like \(2x^2-7x+5=5\) by graphing and finding the intersection of \(f(x) = 2x^2-7x+5\) and \(y=5\)
