  1. False: \(f(x)\) is not \(f\) times \(x\text{.}\) Rather, \(f(x)\) is function notation for the output or \(y\) value. Another notation for the given relation would be \(y = \sqrt{x-4}\text{.}\) \(f(x)\) is equivalent to the \(y\) value, just in a different notation. Dividing both sides of the equation by \(f\) is akin to dividing the function by itself.
