An important thing to notet is that \(f(x)\) does not indicate multiplication between \(f\) and \(x\text{.}\) Rather, \(f(x)\) represents the output or \(y-\)value obtained by plugging the corresponding \(x\) value into the function \(f\text{.}\) A function can be represented algebraically (with a formula), graphically (with a graph), numerically (with a table), or verbally (with a description.)
